Bone Cells make up the skeleton within the bodies of humans and animals. Without the structure and support of bone cells working together, our body would be unable to maintain the pressure of its own weight. Bones also store calcium that can be used by other cells in the body to function properly.

There are three types of Bone Cells that help give bones their strength:

  • Compact Tissue: hard outer layer of bone
  • Subchondral Tissue: smooth tissue at the end of each bone
  • Cancellous Tissue: spongy tissue inside of the compact tissue
