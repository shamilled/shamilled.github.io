All matter is made of atoms and molecules, so what makes the metal chairs that you sit in different from your body?

First off, your body is a Living organism, whereas metal chairs are nonliving. A living organism is anything that requires energy to survive and has Cells. Nonliving objects are made of atoms and molecules grouped together in different ways, like crystals, fibers, or liquids to name a few.

Why are Cells important for living organisms and what is it that they do?

Cells are the microscopic, foundational units of all life. In other words, they are the building blocks used to make all living things. They perform functions like building proteins, fighting off disease, absorbing nutrients, and using energy.
