Volume

    Volume is the amount of three-dimensional space that something takes up.


  • The Base Unit for Volume using the Metric System (SI) is the Liter.

  • Solids, liquids, and gases all have volume.

  • Volume is measured differently for solids, liquids, and gases.


    •       Solids Liquids Gases

Metric System (SI) Units for Volume:

1 Liter (L) = 1000 Milliliters (mL)
1 Milliliter (mL) = 1 Cubic Centimeter (cm3)

Convert U.S. Customary Units to Metric System Units:

1 Gallon (G) = 3.78541 Liters (L)
1 Tablespoon (tbsp.) = 14.7868 Milliliters (mL)
