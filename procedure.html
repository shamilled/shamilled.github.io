Procedure

Materials:

  • Meter Stick
  • Permanent Marker
  • Seeds for Favorite Plant
  • Metric Measuring Cup
  • 2 Ceramic Plant Pots
  • 0.5 Kilogram of Fertilizer 1
  • 0.5 Kilogram of Fertilizer 2
  • 2 Kilograms of Regular Potting Soil
  • 2 Liters of Water

Procedures:

  1. Begin by placing the 2 ceramic Planting Pots near a window or in an area that receives sunlight.
  2. Use the permanent marker to label the first pot as "Plant A" and the second pot "Plant B".
  3. Measure out 1 Kilogram of regular potting soil and pour into the Plant A pot. Repeat this process for the Plant B pot.
  4. Create a small hole in the soil of the Plant A pot and pour 2 seeds into the hole. Cover the hole with soil and pat down.
  5. Repeat step 4 for the Plant B pot using the same type of seeds.
  6. Measure out 0.5 Kilogram of Fertilizer 1 and pour on top of the soil in the Plant A pot. Repeat for the Plant B pot.
  7. Measure out 0.25 Liters of water and pour into Pot A. Repeat for Pot B.
  8. Let the plants sit undisturbed for 24 hours.
  9. After 24 hours have passed, repeat steps 7 and 8.
  10. Use the meter stick to measure the height of both plants using centimeters and record the results into a table.
  11. Repeat steps 11 and 12 everyday for four consecutive days.
