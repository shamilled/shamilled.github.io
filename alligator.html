The stripe pattern on alligator skin and of alligator teeth is because of MATH!

When an alligator is still in its egg, the patterning of its skin starts to develop at the alligator's head. Using math, scientists can predict alligator skin stripes. The space between the stripes on an alligator can be determined by measuring the length and space of the stripes on an alligator and the number of stripes at the hatching of the alligator. See below for an example!

Math example: Below is the math equation for how to figure out the space between stripes on an alligator's skin.

(space between stripes in mm) =
[(tail length) +(neck to rear end length of alligator)]
(Number of stripes at hatching)

Let's use math to determine the space between the stripes on the alligator’s skin below:

We know that the alligator’s tail is 20 millimeters (mm) long. The distance from the neck to the rear end of the alligator is 40 millimeters (mm) long. The alligator had 5 stripes at hatching. So using the math formula above...

(space between stripes in mm) =
[(20 mm + 40 mm)] = 12 mm
5 stripes

...we get 12 millimeters between each stripe.
