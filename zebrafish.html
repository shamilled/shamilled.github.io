Right! The stripes on a zebrafish are because of MATH!

Stripes develop on a zebrafish's skin when the fish is very thin and long. The stripes result from the spreading of the melanin (which turns the skin a color) and an inhibitor (which blocks the spreading of the melanin to the surrounding skin cells).

Can you name two other types of fish that have stripes or spots? What are their names? Where did you see them? If you have an aquarium, look to see if any of your fish have stripes or spots! The patterns are because of math!
